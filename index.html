Viasat Command Line Interface

In my last summer of college, I was invited to join a team at Viasat where I built an internal tool. This was another unique oppurtuntunity where I was able to see a project all the way through its lifecycle. This time, I was working with another intern which was a great experience in itself. At Viasat, I learned the ins and outs of Clojure, remote work, and developing software in an Agile environment.

I began the summer learning about Clojure, a functional programming language and dialect of Lisp. While I had done some functional programming in Javascript, learning Clojure was certainly a paradigm shift. By the end of the summer, we had used it to build a command line tool for viewing and editting the contents of a heirarchical namespace, as well as navigating it like a filesystem. Although we took on most of the CLI work ourselves, my fellow intern and I were a part of a larger Agile team.

After a couple days of learning Clojure and getting set up to work remotely, our project began with a breakdown of an epic. With our manager, we reviewed the expectations of the project and broke down the work into smaller, pointed stories. Each sprint, we took on a few stories and within the two weeks, submitted all of our code, responded to code reviews, and gave demos to a product owner. Because I was working on a team, and our project was bundled into a larger repository, my ability to use git improved dramatically. My manager introduced me to a tool called SmartGit, which was a lifesaver. I still marvel at the fact that we were able to work so smoothly as a team, despite working remotely.

Like many others, the COVID-19 pandemic forced us at Viasat to work from home. Over the course of the summer, I got better at communicating online effectively, drawing the line between work and leisure, and managing my time. Overall, I was surprised at how little being remote affected my ability to learn from peers and work efficiently.

I really enjoyed my summer at Viasat despite the atypical circumstance. The folks that were using the tool we built really appreciate our efforts and emphasized how it would make their jobs a great deal easier.

Lean In HUB

During the fall of my junior year, I was lucky enough to spend a semester abroad in Barcelona working with some great folks over at Dribba. My time at Dribba was dedicated to building an app for Lean In. Despite not having any experience building mobile apps, I was able to get the hang of it using Flutter, a toolkit from Google that uses the Dart programming language. The app debuted at the 2019 Lean In Conference in the Netherlands.

Aside from spending an unforgettable semester in Barcelona, I also took away quite a bit from my time at Dribba. I learned a new programming language, got to work with people around the globe, and built my first mobile app! Even going in to my third year as a computer science major building an app almost entirely from scratch sounded daunting to me. But after a few weeks on the job, some help from my colleagues at Dribba, and countless Google searches, I got used to the process. I loved working on a small project where I could see the changes I was making instantly reflected in the UI and behavior of the app. It was the first time I had built something that people would actually use and that felt pretty great!

If you're interested in learning more about the app, check it out on the App Store or the Google Play Store!

Rowing

When I first arrived at UMass, I knew I wanted to get involved in some sort of sport or club. Luckily for me, some guys from the crew team stopped me on my way in to the dining hall and gave me their pitch. A few years later I can confidentally say that hearing them out was one of the best decisions I've made. Rowing is an incredible sport, but luckily for me their are far better writers that uncover the uncelebrated sport's beauty. On a personal level, there are a few ways I think joining the rowing team has impacted me.

In my few years as a UMass Aggie, I have given up most of my free time, put my body through pain regularly, and DONE ONE OTHER THING. However, none of that even comes close to the joy I have found working with my teammates and coaches towards a common goal, pushing myself to find new limits, and dedicating so much time to something bigger than myself. And on top of all of that, I've developed a brotherly bond with some of the best friends I could ask for.
